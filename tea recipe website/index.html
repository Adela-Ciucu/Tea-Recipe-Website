How to make tea

Tea offers a variety of health benefits. Green, black, white, and oolong teas all contain antioxidants that help reduce oxidative stress in the body. Regular tea consumption has been associated with a longer lifespan. Tea may boost heart health by improving cholesterol levels and reducing the risk of hyperglycemia.Some studies suggest that tea can help lower blood pressure levels.Regular tea consumption has been linked to a reduced risk of stroke.Theaflavins and thearubigins in black tea may contribute to a lower risk of diabetes. Polyphenols in green and black teas are associated with healthy regulation of cancer cell growth and survival. Tea contains compounds that may enhance cognitive function. Tea is a hydrating beverage and a good alternative to water.Certain teas, like chamomile, can help people relax and improve sleep quality.

Remember that while tea provides these benefits, it's essential to maintain a balanced diet and lifestyle for overall wellness. Enjoy your cup of tea!

Ingredients

  • Tea Bag
  • Water
  • Milk - Dairy/Plant based (Optional)
  • Sugar/Honey (Optional)

Preparation

  1. Run the tap a little so the water is nicely aerated, and only boil it once to keep the oxigen level up. Oxigen in water helps flavour!
  2. Pop a tea bag into your mug (always a big mug).
  3. Pour the hot water over the tea bag and stir briefly.
  4. Tea needs time to unlock all its flavours, so give it 3-4 minutes to do its thing. It is a perfect time to a sneaky cookie or daydream about vacantion.
  5. Before removing the tea bag , gently squish it with a spoon against the side of the mug. Not to much or you'll make it bitter.
  6. If you want, throw in some milk or sugar or honey or nothing else at all.
  7. Enjoy!
